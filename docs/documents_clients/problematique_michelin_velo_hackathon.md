# Problématique Michelin Vélo — Hackathon

## 1. Context

- Michelin is a recognized leader in performance and mobility, but in the bicycle market, the brand is not yet a natural choice for cyclists.
- Cycling is rapidly growing across segments: road, MTB, gravel and city.
- This growth is driven by performance, lifestyle and sustainability trends.

**Ambition:** accelerate growth by increasing consumer demand, brand relevance and sales in the short and medium term.

## 2. Core challenge for Michelin

Michelin is facing a vicious cycle:

1. **Low consumer demand** → cyclists do not actively look for Michelin.
2. **Low retailer interest** → limited stock and visibility.
3. **Low availability** → harder to buy.
4. This reinforces low demand.

**Result:** Michelin has strong products, but limited market traction and limited market pull.

## 3. Challenge for the teams

The project must answer two key questions:

- How might we create strong consumer demand and convert it into immediate sales?
- How might we leverage e-retail and digital to make Michelin the easiest and most desirable tyre to buy?

## 4. What success looks like

The solution should:

- Create desire for Michelin among cyclists.
- Deliver short-term sales impact, not only awareness.
- Use e-retail or digital conversion as a key lever.
- Be simple enough to scale quickly.

**Key question:** could this idea generate sales in the next 6 to 12 months?

## 5. Key expectations

The idea must clearly answer:

- How do you create demand?
- How do you convert demand into sales quickly?
- What is the role of e-retail?
- How could this influence or unlock physical retail later?

## 6. Role of e-retail

E-retail is a core part of the solution, not just an option.

It can:

- Remove traditional distribution barriers.
- Enable instant access and conversion.
- Allow demand to be created before physical availability.

The solution should clearly show:

- Where the purchase happens.
- How users move from **interest → click → purchase**.

## 7. Target users

The focus is on **premium cyclists**, especially:

- **Road cyclists:** performance and speed.
- **MTB / gravel cyclists:** control, durability and adventure.
- **Urban cyclists:** safety and reliability.

Priority segments may include:

- Passionate or frequent riders.
- Digital-native consumers.
- Community-driven segments.

## 8. Opportunity areas

Possible areas to explore:

- Demand creation: content, communities, storytelling.
- E-commerce experience: selection, personalization, conversion.
- Influencers, athletes or partnerships.
- Events that drive direct sales.
- New distribution or business models.

## 9. Expected output

Each team deliverable should include:

- A clear idea or concept.
- The consumer insight behind it.
- How it drives demand.
- How it generates short-term sales.
- The e-retail conversion journey.
- A simple go-to-market plan.

## 10. Evaluation criteria from Michelin’s perspective

Michelin will evaluate ideas based on:

1. **Sales impact**, short and medium term, as the number one criterion.
2. Ability to create strong consumer demand.
3. Smart use of e-retail and digital conversion.
4. Differentiation versus competitors.
5. Feasibility and scalability.
6. Creativity.
